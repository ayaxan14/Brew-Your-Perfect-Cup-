Brewing Method Flavour

Brewing Method Flavour
Method How it affects flavour
Drip Produces a clean and smooth cup with balanced strength. The paper filter removes most oils, giving a lighter body.
French Press Results in a full-bodied, bold flavor. The metal filter lets more natural oils and fine particles into the cup, enhancing richness.
AeroPress Creates a smooth, rich coffee with low acidity. It combines pressure and immersion, making a cup similar to espresso but cleaner.

Types of Coffee

This guide has gone through the true fundamentals of coffee, and how it affects the flavour of your soon to-be coffee. Now just like HTML, that was the skeleton structure, now all we need is to apply some CSS, and design our coffee. Here are the several different types of coffees we offer, and their details.

Latte

A latte is made with one shot of espresso and steamed milk, topped with a small amount of milk foam. It’s smooth, creamy, and perfect for those who prefer a milder coffee taste.

Cappuccino

Cappuccino has equal parts espresso, steamed milk, and milk foam. It’s stronger than a latte, with a thick, airy foam layer on top.

Americano

An Americano is made by adding hot water to a shot of espresso. It has a similar strength to drip coffee but with the rich flavor of espresso.

Espresso

A concentrated shot of coffee made by forcing hot water through finely-ground beans. It’s strong, bold, and the base for most other coffee drinks.

Machiatto

This drink means “stained” in Italian. It’s a shot of espresso “stained” with a small amount of milk or milk foam — stronger than a latte or cappuccino.

Flat White

Originating from Australia/New Zealand, a flat white is similar to a latte but smaller, with a thinner layer of microfoam and a stronger espresso taste.

Mocha

A mocha blends espresso, steamed milk, and chocolate syrup or cocoa powder — like a hot chocolate with a caffeine kick.

Cold Brew

Coffee grounds are steeped in cold water for 12 to 24 hours. The result is a smooth, slightly sweet coffee with low acidity — perfect served over ice.

Affogato

A scoop of vanilla ice cream “drowned” in a hot shot of espresso. It’s part dessert, part coffee — and fully delicious.

Iced Coffee

Brewed hot, then cooled and served over ice. Tastes more like traditional drip coffee, but refreshing and chilled.
